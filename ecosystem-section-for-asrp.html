The agentic advertising ecosystem

Where agentic systems are taking hold.

Six entities run the buy. Four already have agentic decision-making operating inside live campaigns. Click any node to read what is live now, and where the signal is moving.

01 · Advertiser · Brands

How advertisers are deploying and preparing for agent-driven buying.

Live now

Major brand-side teams in ANZ run PMax and Advantage+ at scale, with most of the bid logic delegated to the platforms. In-house programmatic trading desks remain rare; spend flows through agencies or platform-direct. First-party data activation through clean rooms is growing faster than the measurement maturity to validate it.

Where the signal is moving

Brand-side teams are starting to demand decisioning transparency from the platforms. Procurement is writing agentic-aware clauses into MSA renewals. Internal capability builds are tilting toward clean-room operation and platform-direct API work, not toward traditional trading hours.

02 · Intermediary · Agencies

The changing role of the agency inside an automated buying stack.

Live now

Holding companies are deploying their own agent layers above the platform agents. WPP Open, Publicis CoreAI, Omnicom and IPG following. Independent agencies are under margin pressure as platform automation displaces trading hours that used to be billable.

Where the signal is moving

The agency contract is being rewritten to handle algorithmic spend management. The FTE-billed model is breaking against the reality that most optimisation work has moved into platform agents. Independents are repositioning around strategy, measurement and procurement advisory, where the operator judgment still pays.

03 · Buy-side · Buying Platforms

DSPs and demand infrastructure embedding agentic decision-making.

Live now

Google PMax and Meta Advantage+ are operating live in ANZ campaigns. PMax decides creative, audience, placement and budget allocation across YouTube, Search, Discovery, Gmail and Display. Advantage+ Shopping does the same on Meta surfaces. The Trade Desk launched Koa Agents in alpha in April 2026, with Stagwell as first partner, on top of its existing Koa bidding intelligence.

Where the signal is moving

Platform agents are starting to operate as autonomous campaign creators. Brief to launch, no human in the loop. Bid-stream decisioning is being abstracted away from human traders entirely. The interesting question is no longer "how do I tune the algorithm" but "how do I run alongside one I cannot tune."

04 · Intelligence · Data Platforms

Measurement, identity and modelling becoming the agent's nervous system.

Live now

Identity resolution and clean-room infrastructure (LiveRamp, Habu, Snowflake, AWS Clean Rooms) underpins the data layer that platform agents pull from. Measurement vendors (Innovid, IAS, DoubleVerify, Nielsen) are embedding ML into outcome models. MMM is having a renaissance because attribution can no longer carry the load.

Where the signal is moving

Whoever owns identity resolution and clean-room infrastructure becomes the connective tissue between platform agents. Measurement is becoming the only honest broker, because every platform agent will report itself favourably. The procurement question shifts from "which DSP" to "which measurement vendor will I let mark the homework."

05 · Sell-side · Supply Platforms

SSPs building agent interfaces as protocol wars reshape inventory access.

Live now

SSPs (PubMatic, Magnite, OpenX) are building agent interfaces and protocols. PubMatic launched AgenticOS in January 2026 and confirmed live end-to-end agentic campaigns in Australia in April 2026. Curation marketplaces are moving up the stack. Header bidding still dominates for legacy reasons, but agentic supply paths are gaining share.

Where the signal is moving

Protocol wars. The fight is over who controls the bid-request schema that agentic buyers transact against. SSPs that make their inventory legible to buy-side agents win. SSPs that don't get skipped, regardless of price.

06 · Destination · Publishers

Content owners adapting to agent-mediated discovery and direct deal negotiation.

Live now

Most ANZ publishers still optimise for human-trader workflows. Programmatic Guaranteed and PMP deals dominate. Most inventory is not structured for agentic discoverability. Some larger publishers (News Corp, Nine, Seven West) are building first-party data and clean-room infrastructure to remain transactable.

Where the signal is moving

Publishers are being skipped by agentic systems that cannot parse their inventory. Direct-deal infrastructure is becoming the only path back to the buy-side for non-Google, non-Meta supply. Content licensing for AI training is emerging as a parallel revenue line that will reshape what content gets made.
